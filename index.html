Welcome

I am a UKCP and BACP registered psychotherapist in Bayswater (W2), Maida Vale (W9), and online. I offer therapy in English, Mandarin, and Cantonese.

Therapy provides a confidential space to explore what feels stuck, overwhelming, or unclear in your life, and where meaningful change can begin.

How therapy works

Through our conversations, you may discover new ways of understanding yourself and responding to life's challenges. My role is to support you with empathy and attention as patterns shift and clarity emerges.

Getting started

I offer a reduced-fee initial consultation (50 minutes, £35) to discuss what brings you to therapy. Contact me by email (jennifer.wei.du@gmail.com) or text (07840 232 467).

How do you work?

I offer a relational, person-centred approach. Rather than following a set programme, I stay close to what you bring, trusting that understanding and change grow through being listened to carefully.

How often will I need to come to therapy?

Most people come weekly. We can adjust frequency based on your needs.

What happens in a psychotherapy session?

Sessions are 50 minutes in a confidential space. You bring whatever feels important — concerns, relationships, memories, or difficult feelings. There's no pressure to talk in a particular way.

Are you regulated by a professional body?

Yes. I am registered with UKCP and BACP, working in line with their ethical frameworks including confidentiality, professional boundaries, and data protection (CID registered).

Are you in personal therapy yourself?

Yes. Personal therapy supports my capacity to remain open, grounded, and attentive in my work.

What supervision do you undertake?

I attend fortnightly clinical supervision to ensure safe, ethical practice.

How do you maintain your professional development?

I engage in ongoing Continuing Professional Development (CPD) to stay connected to current thinking and continue developing as a therapist.

Do you have a cancellation policy?

Yes. I ask for one week's notice for cancellations or changes to sessions.
